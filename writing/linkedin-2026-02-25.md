# LinkedIn Post — February 25, 2026

---

Today I shipped three things to a live domain I own: a Medicare 8-Minute Rule calculator with a full automated test suite, a landing page for arcanarium.me, and a PT note template library with one-click clipboard copy.

I am a physical therapist. I have never worked in tech.

The calculator wasn't a toy project. Billing math has compliance consequences, so before anything went live we wrote 13 automated tests — one for each CMS scenario in the manual — and didn't ship until they all passed.

What made this possible isn't just that AI can generate code. It's that I have a tutor that starts from wherever I am. When I asked what a JSX file was, I got a clear, jargon-free answer and we kept moving. When I asked the same question twice because the first answer didn't fully land, I got a better answer with no friction. There's no social cost to not knowing something. That changes what you're willing to try.

I wrote about the learning side of this in more depth here: [link to blog post]

If you're a clinician, a teacher, or anyone with ideas and no technical background — the barrier to building real things is lower than it's ever been. Not because it's easy, but because for the first time you have a tutor with infinite patience and no condescension.
